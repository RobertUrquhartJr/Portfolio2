I'm Robert Urquhart Jr.

My coding adventure started in the early 2000s during the Myspace era, weaving through glittery backgrounds and custom HTML. This curiosity evolved into problem-solving during adulthood as I explored the world of 'rooting' cell phones and infusing them with personal flair. While I wasn't the one writing the code, it fascinated me that someone possessed the skill to do so and generously shared it with the world.



Recently attaining a degree in Information Technology and backed by nearly two decades of diverse professional experience, my knack for navigating challenges gracefully led me to the decision of transitioning into software development. Coding, for me, is a meticulous art, a source of satisfaction in debugging and optimizing for seamless functionality. This devotion to crafting elegant solutions is grounded in a natural curiosity and a deep passion for conquering complex challenges. Untangling complexities and turning them into innovative opportunities is where I find joy. An integral part of my approach is the fervent drive for creation, propelling my commitment to transforming ideas into tangible, well-crafted solutions.

Kind, Not Nice Camp

Kind, Not Nice Camp is a full-stack web application developed in Colt Steele's Web Developer Bootcamp. The application allows users to create a user profile and has login and logout functionality. While logged in a user is able to add, edit and delete campgrounds along with add or delete images. Interactive maps allow users to view the location of campgrounds
